# Quantium Customer Segment & Chips Sales Analysis

## Project Overview

This project was completed as part of the Quantium Virtual Internship program.

The objective was to analyze retail transaction data and customer purchase behaviour to identify key sales drivers, customer segments, and commercial opportunities within the chips category.

Using SQL, Excel, and Power BI, raw transactional data was cleaned, transformed, analyzed, and converted into business recommendations for the Category Manager.

---

## Business Objective

Provide data-driven recommendations to improve category performance by understanding:

- Which customer segments drive revenue
- Which brands perform best
- Which pack sizes are preferred
- What factors influence customer spending

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Kettle Was the Top Revenue Brand

Kettle consistently generated the highest revenue across multiple customer segments.

### 2. Older Families Had Highest Spend per Customer

This segment showed the strongest average customer spend, indicating high-value shoppers.

### 3. Young Singles/Couples (Mainstream) Had Largest Customer Base

A major growth opportunity due to scale and strong purchase frequency.

### 4. 175g Packs Drove Highest Revenue

This was the most preferred pack size across key customer groups.

### 5. Customer Segments Behave Differently

Spending patterns varied significantly by lifestage and premium segment.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

- Increase visibility and shelf placement for Kettle products
- Use targeted promotions for Older Families
- Build campaigns for Young Singles/Couples
- Prioritize 175g pack availability
- Use segment-based pricing and assortment strategies
